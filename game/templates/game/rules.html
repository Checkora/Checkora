♟ Chess Rules & Guide ♟

How to Play

Welcome to Checkora, a platform for playing chess online. Here are the basic rules and features of the game.

Piece Movement

  • Pawn: Moves forward one square (or two squares from starting position). Captures diagonally.
  • Rook: Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
  • Knight: Moves in an L-shape (2 squares in one direction, 1 square perpendicular).
  • Bishop: Moves diagonally any number of squares.
  • Queen: Moves any number of squares in any direction (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally).
  • King: Moves one square in any direction. Can also castle under specific conditions.

Game Objectives

  • Checkmate: The primary goal is to checkmate the opponent's king (put it under attack with no legal moves).
  • Check: An attack on the king that must be responded to immediately.
  • Stalemate: A draw occurs when a player cannot move and is not in check.
  • Draw: Can occur by agreement, insufficient material, threefold repetition, or fifty-move rule.

Special Moves

  • Castling: A special move where the king and rook move simultaneously. Only allowed if neither piece has moved and squares between them are empty and not under attack.
  • En Passant: A special pawn capture that can occur when an opponent's pawn moves two squares forward from its starting position.
  • Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opponent's back rank, it promotes to a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.

Game Modes

  • Player vs Player (PvP): Play against another player on the same board.
  • Player vs AI: Play against the computer engine with adjustable difficulty levels (Easy, Medium, Hard).

Features

  • Hints: Get AI suggestions for your next move (limited to 3 per game).
  • Time Control: Games have a configurable time limit (default: 10 minutes per player).
  • Move History: Review all moves made during the game in algebraic notation.
  • Statistics: Track your win/loss ratio against the AI and other players.

Tips for Playing

  • Control the center of the board in the opening.
  • Develop your pieces early and move your king to safety by castling.
  • Protect your pieces and avoid unnecessary trades.
  • Look ahead for tactical opportunities and threats.
  • Practice with the AI to improve your skills.
